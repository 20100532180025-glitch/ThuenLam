The Wonders of Bhutan

Explore the breathtaking places in the kingdom of Bhutan!

Paro Taktsang

One of the places, considered one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in the Himalayas, it is believed to be where Guru Rinpoche (Padmasambhava) introduced Buddhism to Bhutan.As the "Tiger's Nest," it carries the essence of Bhutanese culture and is a deeply significant symbol for its people, often representing the country globally.

Phobjikha Valley

A famous site for the sightings of Black-Necked Cranes. A breathtaking place best for relaxtion and vaction. The valleys are mesmerizing and beautiful.

Buddha Dordenma

A gigantic Shakyamuni Buddha statue in the mountains of Bhutan. It ranks among the largest sitting Buddha statues in the world.

Punakha Suspension Bridge

Stretching across the majestic Pho Chu river, the Punakha suspension bridge is 180 m in length, making it one of the longest suspension bridges in the country. It offers a great view of the glistening stream below.

Dochula Pass

The Dochu La is a mountain pass in the snow covered Himalayas in Bhutan on the road from Thimphu to Punakha. 108 memorial chorten were built at the pass by Ashi Dorji Wangmo Wangchuk, the eldest Queen Mother, in memory of Bhutanese soldiers who died in 2003. On clear mornings, you can see the majestic snow-capped Himayalan range.

Ta Dzong

Ta Dzong, national museum of Bhutan, features the country's rich cultural and spiritual heritage.
